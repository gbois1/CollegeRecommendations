3. University of California, Berkely


Offical Website:

https://www.berkeley.edu/

  • Average cost after aid: $15k
  • Graduation rate: 92%
  • Acceptance rate: 16%
  • Location: Berkeley, CA

To start off, The Center for Access to Engineering Excellence (CAEE) at Berkeley is committed to providing a supportive and welcoming environment conducive to academic and personal success.They pride themselves as champions for diversity, equity, and inclusion in all of their programs and services. Secondly, you'll meet new people, create professional connections, and expand your skill sets along with finding more than 850 clubs and leadership opportunities. At Berkeley, you are encouraged to bring your authentic self, your craziest concepts, your own distinct upbringing, and your belief system to incorporate into your work.

2. Stanford University


Offical Website:

https://www.stanford.edu/

  • Average cost after aid: $12k
  • Graduation rate: 94%
  • Acceptance rate: 4%
  • Location: Stanford, CA

The School of Engineering's mission is to conduct groundbreaking research that includes and addresses many different problems in the world and to educate future engineers to meet challenges specific to the 21st century. Stanford takes pride in its diverse group of faculty, students, and staff that makes the school a safe space for individuals from a variety of backgrounds and wants to continue creating diversity. Stanford Engineering offers 16 defined undergraduate majors, plus the option to design your own. Stanford also has a bright campus, hundreds of student groups that help you connect, and access to research opportunities and mentorship.

1. Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)


Offical Website:

https://www.mit.edu/

  • Average cost after aid: $18k
  • Graduation rate: 93%
  • Acceptance rate: 7%
  • Location: Cambridge, MA

About 45% of all graduate students at MIT are enrolled in the School of Engineering and graduate students have 15 areas in which to earn degrees, most of which are highly ranked. The MIT community is driven by a shared purpose: to make a better world through education, research, and innovation. New Engineering Education Transformation (NEET) at MIT allows students to have unparalleled opportunities to immerse themselves in projects that push their minds out of the box while earning a degree in their chosen major.Learning at MIT is very hands-on and MIT pushes its students to take on greater challenges headfirst and not be afraid of making mistakes.
